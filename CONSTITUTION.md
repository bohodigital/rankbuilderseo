# Rank Builder SEO Constitution v1.0

## 1. Identity

Rank Builder SEO exists to publish source-heavy SEO research, pricing clarity, provider reviews, audit reviews, technical guides, experiment logs, and bad-pattern writeups for readers who want receipts instead of rituals.

It is an independent SEO research lab and buyer-defense publication.

It is not an agency landing page, lead funnel, affiliate farm, fake review directory, or AI slop content mill.

## 2. No SEO Service Advertising

The site must not advertise SEO services on published pages.

Do not add:

- hire-us language
- book-a-call CTAs
- lead forms framed as editorial content
- agency sales copy
- soft-pitch conversion funnels disguised as guides

If monetization is reviewed later, the review must happen after trust, disclosure, and conflict rules are mature.

## 3. Source-First Research

Rank Builder publishes from evidence before opinion.

Primary and official sources should be checked first whenever they exist.

Secondary commentary can add context, but it must not replace the underlying source record.

If a claim cannot be supported yet, narrow the claim or mark the work as draft.

## 4. Citation Doctrine

Meaningful factual claims should be traceable to a source note, source list, experiment log, or clearly stated internal observation.

Facts, inference, and opinion must be separated.

Source notes should make it clear whether the site is citing:

- official vendor documentation
- public product or pricing pages
- first-hand testing
- public statements
- standards documentation
- archived or historical materials

Do not hide weak sourcing behind confident tone.

## 5. Provider Review Rules

Provider reviews must not pretend to be neutral if the site has a conflict.

Every provider review should state:

- what was reviewed
- what was not reviewed
- what evidence was available
- what evidence was missing
- what claims were verified directly
- what conclusions remain provisional

Do not invent scores, stars, rankings, awards, or comparative claims without a published method and supporting evidence.

## 6. Audit Review Rules

Audit reviews are evaluations of audit quality, not theater.

Every audit review should explain:

- scope
- inputs reviewed
- whether implementation proof existed
- whether claims were reproducible
- what was technically accurate
- what was vague, unsupported, or over-sold

Do not imply fraud, malpractice, or incompetence without durable evidence.

## 7. Named Company Callout Rules

Named companies may be discussed only when there is a clear factual basis.

Allowed use cases:

- quoting a public pricing page
- reviewing a published provider claim
- documenting a public product behavior
- citing a public support or documentation source
- reviewing an audit that the publisher has rights to discuss

Do not publish reckless accusations, invented motives, or guilt-by-association claims.

When evidence is incomplete, say that evidence is incomplete.

## 8. Pricing Philosophy

Pricing coverage exists to reduce buyer confusion and expose ambiguity, not to create outrage bait.

Pricing pages should explain:

- common billing models
- what buyers are actually paying for
- what usually changes the price
- what should be clarified before signing
- where the price claim came from

Do not publish fake market averages or invented benchmark tables.

## 9. Anti-Bullshit Rules

Rank Builder does not publish:

- fake testimonials
- fake logos
- fake proof
- fake case studies
- fake review stars
- fake awards
- fake provider rankings
- fake urgency
- fake guarantees
- fake authority by tone alone

If the evidence is thin, the page should read as thin.

If the conclusion is limited, the page should state the limit.

## 10. Experiment Standards

Experiments must be reversible, logged, and scoped tightly enough to learn something real.

Each experiment should identify:

- hypothesis
- change under test
- pages affected
- baseline state
- measurement window
- limitations
- what result would not prove

No experiment log should claim more than the observed evidence supports.

## 11. Technical Standards

The site should remain:

- static-first
- dependency-light
- readable without JavaScript
- crawlable
- linkable
- source-controlled
- reproducible

Generated files must be buildable locally, validated before publication, and committed when production depends on committed output.

## 12. AI Use Rules

AI may assist with drafting, outlining, formatting, extraction, and code generation.

AI must not be treated as a source.

AI-generated text must be reviewed before publication for:

- invented facts
- unsupported confidence
- washed-out language
- fake specificity
- citation gaps

If AI helped produce a page, the page still has to meet the same sourcing and verification standard as fully human-written work.

## 13. Agent Operating Rules

Agents working in this repo must:

- inspect the live repo before editing
- preserve existing working URLs unless replaced deliberately
- keep Cloudflare Pages compatibility intact
- avoid unnecessary dependencies
- keep drafts out of public output
- fail loudly on malformed published content
- stage only task-owned files
- commit with a reviewable message that states why, what changed, validation performed, and remaining risk

Agents must not smuggle service advertising into editorial pages.

## 14. Launch Definition

Rank Builder SEO is launch-ready only when:

- the publishing shell exists
- the editorial rules are documented
- published routes are clean and navigable
- source-backed starter content exists
- sitemap and RSS are generated from metadata
- legacy URLs are preserved or redirected
- drafts stay private
- validation passes
- the public site reads like a research lab, not an agency funnel
