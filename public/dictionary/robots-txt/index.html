robots.txt

A short definition of robots.txt and how it helps communicate crawl rules to automated agents.

Published July 01, 2026 | Updated July 01, 2026

Short answer

robots.txt is a public text file that gives crawl instructions to automated agents at the site level. It helps communicate what may or may not be crawled, but it is not a private access control system.

Why it matters

Because the file is public and easy to inspect, it is one of the first places to check when a site unexpectedly blocks or exposes crawl paths.

How to verify

  • Open /robots.txt directly.
  • Confirm the intended crawl rules are present and readable.
  • Check whether the sitemap location is declared when appropriate.

Sources and notes

  • Google Search Central documentation is a primary reference for crawler-facing robots handling.
