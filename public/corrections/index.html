Corrections Policy

How Rank Builder SEO handles factual corrections, updates, and disputed claims.

Published July 01, 2026 | Updated July 01, 2026

Short answer

When a published page contains a factual error, an outdated claim, or an unclear statement that changes the reader's understanding, Rank Builder should correct it plainly and preserve the fact that a correction occurred.

Why it matters

Research credibility does not come from pretending mistakes never happen. It comes from making corrections visible, scoped, and traceable.

How to verify

If you are challenging a claim, keep the request concrete:

  1. Identify the page URL.
  2. Quote the exact sentence or section at issue.
  3. Provide the better source, missing context, or reproducible technical proof.
  4. State whether the issue is factual, contextual, or interpretive.

Sources and notes

  • Corrections should distinguish between factual fixes, scope clarifications, and opinion disagreements.
  • If a page changes materially, the modified date should move with it.
